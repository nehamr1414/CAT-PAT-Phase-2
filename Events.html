Events

This page shows some of the major climate events impacting South Africa and the overall effects of climate change on South African citizens and communities.

The 2022 Durban Floods

The Durban floods of April 2022 were a devastating event, causing widespread damage and loss of life. Heavy rainfall led to severe flooding, landslides, and infrastructure damage. Over 400 people lost their lives, and thousands were displaced from their homes. The floods highlighted the vulnerability of urban areas to extreme weather events, caused by climate change. 12000 homes were damaged or destroyed, and critical infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and power lines were severely affected. The economic impact was significant, with damages estimated at over R20 billion. The disaster prompted a massive response from government agencies, NGOs, and the international community. Efforts focused on rescue operations, providing emergency relief, and rebuilding affected communities. The floods underscored the urgent need for improved urban planning, better drainage systems, and enhanced disaster preparedness to mitigate the impacts of future climate-related events. There was a total of 300mm of rainfall recorded during the floods.

The Cape Town Water Crisis - "Day Zero"

The Cape Town water crisis is a significant issue caused by climate change, with the city facing severe water shortages due to prolonged droughts and mismanagement of water resources. In 2018, Cape Town came dangerously close to "Day Zero," when the city would have run out of water entirely. The crisis highlighted the need for sustainable water management practices and increased resilience to climate impacts. The drought was deemed one of the worst in the city's history, prompting urgent action from local authorities and residents alike. Residents were limited to only 50 liters of water per person per day, forcing them to adopt drastic water-saving measures. Climate change played a huge factor as one of the causes of this drought. Decreased rainfall patterns and higher temperatures contributed to the water scarcity, making it essential for the city to adapt to the changing climate.

Heatwaves in Johannesburg

Johannesburg has experienced a rise in the frequency and intensity of heatwaves, with temperatures soaring above 35Â°C during summer months. These heatwaves pose significant health risks, particularly for vulnerable populations such as the elderly and those with pre-existing health conditions. The urban heat island effect worsened the situation, as built-up areas retain heat and create localized temperature increases. In response, the city has implemented measures to improve green spaces, increase tree cover, and promote sustainable building practices to mitigate the impacts of extreme heat. Public awareness campaigns have also been launched to educate residents about the dangers of heatwaves and the importance of staying hydrated and cool. The rise in temperatures is caused by climate change and is worsening the overall climate crisis.

Impact on Vulnerable Communities

Climate change disproportionately affects vulnerable communities in South Africa, particularly those living in informal settlements and rural areas. These communities often lack access to basic services such as clean water, sanitation, and healthcare, making them more susceptible to the impacts of climate change. Extreme weather events, such as floods and droughts, can devastate livelihoods, destroy homes, and increase poverty levels. Due to their poor drainage systems, rural communities are more at risk of flooding and heavy storms can lead to waterborne diseases, food insecurity, and displacement. As the poverty and unemployment rates are already high in South Africa, climate change worsens these issues, making it harder for vulnerable populations to adapt and recover from climate-related disasters.
