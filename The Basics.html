Getting Familiar with Climate Change

What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term alteration of weather patterns, temperatures, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. It is primarily driven by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, which increase the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Why is Climate Change Important?

Climate change poses significant risks to the environment, human health, and economies worldwide. It can lead to more frequent and severe weather events, rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems and agriculture.

Key Concepts

  • Greenhouse Gases (GHGs): These are gases that trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to the greenhouse effect. The main GHGs include carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O).
  • Global Warming: This refers to the increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to rising levels of GHGs.
  • Carbon Footprint: This is the total amount of GHG emissions caused directly and indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product.
  • Renewable Energy: Energy sources that are naturally replenished, such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power, which help reduce GHG emissions.
