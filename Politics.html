Policies and Governance

This page provides an overview of key policies and governance issues in South Africa, focusing on the government's role in combating climate change.

Role of the Local Government

Focusing on the major cities of South Africa - Cape Town, Johannesburg, and Durban - the local government plays a crucial role in addressing climate change at the community level. They are responsible for implementing national policies and adapting them to local contexts. In Cape Town, they have implemented water saving strategies such as water restrictions and public awareness campaigns to combat the water crisis. They have also invested in renewable energy projects, such as solar and wind power, to reduce the city's carbon footprint. In Johannesburg, they have focused on improving public transportation, like the Gautrain for example, to reduce traffic congestion and emissions. They have also implemented climate resilience policies to protect vulnerable communities from the impacts of extreme weather events. Durban has prioritized coastal management and flood prevention measures to address the risks posed by rising sea levels and extreme storms. In 2021, Durban launched the Durban Climate Change Strategy, which outlines the city's approach to climate adaptation and mitigation. The local governments in these cities work closely with community organizations, businesses, and residents to promote sustainable practices and build resilience to climate change impacts.

Policies to Combat Climate Change

We'll focus on two policies implemented in South Africa to combat climate change: the National Climate Change Response White Paper (NCCRWP) and the Just Energy Transition (JET) investment plan. The NCCRWP emphasizes the importance of reducing greenhouse gas emissions, adapting to climate impacts, and integrating climate considerations into national planning and decision-making processes. It also highlights the need for collaboration between government, civil society, and the private sector to achieve its goals. The policy sets out specific targets for emission reductions and adaptation measures, as well as strategies for financing climate action and building capacity at all levels of government. The NCCRWP serves as a guiding document for South Africa's efforts to combat climate change and transition to a low-carbon, climate-resilient economy. The JET investment plan aims to facilitate the transition to a sustainable energy future by promoting renewable energy projects, enhancing energy efficiency, and creating green jobs.

South Africa has progressed in the fight against climate change but still faces significant challenges. The impacts of climate change are already being felt, particularly in vulnerable communities that lack the resources to adapt.
